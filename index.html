CV Essentials (M³)

Keep it 1–2 pages

Every impact line should include a number, the mechanism you touched, and why it mattered. Example:

“Reduced LCP 3.9s → 1.8s (−54%) in 5 weeks via Next.js SSR & image CDN; +6% completed sign‑ups.”
  • Metric: from→to delta, timeframe, source (e.g., Datadog, BigQuery, Lighthouse).
  • Mechanism: what you changed — architecture, patterns, controls.
  • Meaning: tie it to users or the business.

ATS‑friendly skeleton

FULL NAME — Title (e.g., Senior Engineer)
City · Email · Phone · LinkedIn · GitHub

SUMMARY — 2–4 lines tailored to the role (stack, scale, 1–2 measurable outcomes)

CORE SKILLS — your real core, not a dictionary

EXPERIENCE
Company — Role (YYYY–YYYY)
• Impact bullet (M³)
• Depth bullet (trade‑offs, reliability, observability)
• Collaboration/mentoring

EDUCATION & CERTS — optional
PROJECTS — optional, if relevant

Interview quick‑prep

  • Prepare a 5‑minute system design story with diagrams.
  • Own a failure story and the fix (runbooks, SLOs, tests).
  • Have 3 M³ bullets you’re proud of.
  • Clarify what you want next: learning, impact, team.

Behavioral scaffold

STAR works; I prefer PRIME — Problem, Reasoning, Impact, Method, Evidence.

“Problem: flaky deploys → Reasoning: missing test pyramid → Method: trunk‑based + CI gates → Impact: incidents −40% → Evidence: dashboards.”

Anti‑fluff checklist

  • Replace “helped”, “worked on” → with measurable verbs.
  • Cut tool laundry lists; emphasize depth and trade‑offs.
  • If you don’t know, say so and show how you’d find out.

Salary & Offer Playbook

  • Know your walk‑away and your BATNA.
  • Negotiate package, not just salary: base, bonus, equity, WFH budget, education.
  • Ask for leveling rubric and growth path in writing.
  • Align start date with vesting or bonus timelines.

Offer evaluation checklist

  • Team quality: manager, peer loop signals.
  • Problem fit: do you want these problems for 2 years?
  • Learning pace: seniors to learn from? time for craft?
  • Life fit: remote, timezones, flexibility.

Negotiation scripts

“I\'m excited. Given my impact in X and market data at Y–Z, can we explore a base in A–B? I\'m ready to sign if we can align on this.”

Portfolio that lands interviews

  • Short case studies: problem → approach → measurable result.
  • Diagrams over paragraphs; code snippets sparingly.
  • Live demos or GIFs; README with quick start.

Project story template

Context → Constraints → Options → Decision → Result (with numbers) → Lessons

Tech writing mini‑guide

  • One idea per page. Title = promise of value.
  • Examples > assertions. Show your work.
  • Prefer diagrams + bullets + code comments.

Networking that works

Reach out with value. A simple DM template:

Hi <Name>, loved your post on <topic>. I built <small demo> exploring <idea>. Would you be open to a 15‑min chat? Either way, here\'s the repo: <link> — Cheers!
  • Comment with substance; avoid generic “great post”.
  • Show curiosity; ask concrete questions.
  • Give before you ask.

Remote comms, done right

  • Asynchronous first: clear titles, TL;DR, decision logs.
  • Disagree & commit: capture decisions in writing.
  • Own your metrics; publish dashboards.
